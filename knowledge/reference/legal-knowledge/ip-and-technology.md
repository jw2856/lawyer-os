# IP & Technology

## IP Fundamentals

### Types of IP in Technology Agreements
- **Patents**: Protect inventions and methods (including software/business methods in some jurisdictions)
- **Copyrights**: Protect expression (code, documentation, creative works) — automatic, no registration required
- **Trade secrets**: Protect confidential business information — requires reasonable measures to maintain secrecy
- **Trademarks**: Protect brand identifiers (names, logos, slogans)

### Ownership Concepts
- **Pre-existing IP**: What each party brings to the agreement — typically retained by the originating party
- **Developed IP**: Created during the agreement — ownership must be explicitly addressed
- **Work-for-hire**: Under US law, works created by employees are employer's IP; for contractors, only certain categories qualify and must be explicitly agreed
- **Assignment vs license**: Assignment = transfer of ownership; License = permission to use (can be exclusive/non-exclusive, perpetual/term, etc.)

## SaaS/Technology Agreement Patterns

### Key Terms
- **License scope**: Non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable is standard for SaaS
- **Usage restrictions**: Reverse engineering, benchmarking, competitive analysis prohibitions
- **Customizations**: Who owns custom development? Must be explicit.
- **Source code escrow**: For critical dependencies — access if vendor becomes insolvent/ceases operations
- **Data portability**: Right to extract your data on termination
- **API terms**: Rate limits, versioning, breaking change notification requirements

### Open Source
- **Permissive licenses** (MIT, Apache 2.0, BSD): Generally safe for commercial use
- **Copyleft licenses** (GPL, AGPL): Can require source code disclosure — review carefully
- **License compatibility**: Mixing licenses can create obligations
- Monitor vendor tech stacks for open source obligations

## Technology-Specific Contract Issues
- **Uptime and availability** commitments (SLAs)
- **Data backup and recovery** obligations
- **Security controls** and certification requirements
- **Integration** rights and obligations
- **Transition assistance** on termination
- **Vendor lock-in** risks and mitigation
