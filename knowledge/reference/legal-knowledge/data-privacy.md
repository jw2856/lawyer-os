# Data Privacy

## Key Regulations

### GDPR (EU/EEA)
- Applies to processing of personal data of EU/EEA residents
- Key requirements: lawful basis, data minimization, purpose limitation, data subject rights
- Controller vs processor distinction critical in agreements
- DPA required when sharing personal data with processors
- 72-hour breach notification to supervisory authority
- Cross-border transfers: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or adequacy decisions
- Fines up to 4% of global annual turnover or EUR 20M

### CCPA/CPRA (California)
- Applies to personal information of California residents
- Business vs service provider distinction (analogous to controller/processor)
- Right to delete, right to know, right to opt-out of sale/sharing
- Data processing agreements needed with service providers

### US State Privacy Laws
- Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Connecticut (CTDPA), and growing
- Generally follow GDPR-lite model with consumer rights
- Varying thresholds for applicability

### Other Major Regimes
- Brazil LGPD — GDPR-influenced, applies to processing of Brazilian residents' data
- Singapore PDPA — Consent-based, managed by PDPC
- Kenya Data Protection Act — Applies to processing within Kenya
- UK GDPR — Post-Brexit, largely mirrors EU GDPR

## Key Concepts

### Controller vs Processor
- **Controller**: Determines the purposes and means of processing
- **Processor**: Processes data on behalf of the controller
- **Joint controllers**: Two or more controllers jointly determine purposes/means
- Classification determines who has what obligations and liability

### DPA Requirements (Standard)
- Processing description (what data, what purpose, what categories of data subjects)
- Security measures (technical and organizational)
- Breach notification timeline
- Sub-processor requirements and controls
- International transfer mechanisms (SCCs if applicable)
- Audit rights
- Data return/deletion obligations on termination

### Breach Notification Timelines
- GDPR: 72 hours to supervisory authority
- CCPA: "Most expedient time possible" / "without unreasonable delay"
- LGPD: "Reasonable time" (ANPD recommends 2 business days)
- Contractual: Often tighter than regulatory (24-48 hours)

### Cross-Border Transfers
- EU → adequate countries: No additional safeguards needed
- EU → non-adequate countries: SCCs, BCRs, or other approved mechanism
- US data transfers: State-specific requirements emerging
