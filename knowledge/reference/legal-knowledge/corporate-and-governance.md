# Corporate & Governance

## Entity Types
- **Corporation** (Inc., Corp.): Separate legal entity, shareholders, board of directors, officers
- **LLC**: Flexible structure, members, can be member-managed or manager-managed
- **Limited Partnership**: General and limited partners, common for investment vehicles
- **Foreign entity registration**: Required when operating in states other than state of formation

## Governance Basics
- **Board of directors**: Fiduciary duties (duty of care, duty of loyalty)
- **Officers**: Day-to-day management, authority to bind the company
- **Signing authority**: Who can sign agreements and at what thresholds
- **Board approvals**: Certain actions (M&A, large contracts, equity grants) may require board approval

## Intercompany Agreements
- Required when related entities transact with each other
- Transfer pricing considerations
- Arm's length standard
- Documentation requirements

## Employment Fundamentals
- **At-will employment**: Default in most US states (exception: Montana)
- **Contractor classification**: ABC test (California AB5), IRS factors, economic reality test
- **Non-compete enforceability**: Varies dramatically by state — banned in California, limited in many states
- **IP assignment**: Typically in employment agreements; some states restrict (California Labor Code 2870)
- **Equity compensation**: Stock options, RSUs — securities law implications

## M&A / Investment Basics
- **Due diligence**: Legal, financial, operational review of target
- **Representations and warranties**: Seller's statements about the business
- **Indemnification**: Post-closing protection for breaches of reps
- **Material adverse change**: Conditions that could allow deal termination
