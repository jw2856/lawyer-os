# Litigation & Disputes

## Dispute Resolution Mechanisms
- **Negotiation**: Direct discussion between parties — always the first step
- **Mediation**: Neutral third party facilitates resolution — non-binding
- **Arbitration**: Neutral arbitrator(s) decide — binding, limited appeal rights
  - Key institutions: AAA, JAMS (US), LCIA (London), ICC (Paris), SIAC (Singapore)
  - Advantages: confidential, faster, specialized arbitrators
  - Disadvantages: limited discovery, limited appeal, can be expensive
- **Litigation**: Court proceedings — binding, full appeal rights, public record

## Litigation Holds
- **Trigger**: When litigation is reasonably anticipated (not just when filed)
- **Obligation**: Preserve all potentially relevant documents and data
- **Scope**: Electronic and physical documents, including emails, chats, files
- **Process**: Issue hold notice, suspend auto-deletion policies, confirm compliance
- **Failure to preserve**: Spoliation sanctions — adverse inference, monetary penalties

## Discovery Obligations
- **US federal**: FRCP Rules 26-37 govern discovery
- **Proportionality**: Discovery must be proportional to the needs of the case
- **Privilege**: Attorney-client privilege and work product doctrine protect certain communications
- **ESI**: Electronically stored information — specialized rules and challenges

## Key Concepts for Contract Disputes
- **Material breach**: Breach that goes to the essence of the agreement
- **Cure period**: Time to fix a breach before termination rights trigger
- **Notice requirements**: Proper notice is often a precondition to exercising remedies
- **Limitation periods**: Statute of limitations varies by claim type and jurisdiction
- **Damages**: Direct, consequential, incidental — contractual limitations apply
- **Specific performance**: Court-ordered performance (rare, typically for unique obligations)
- **Injunctive relief**: Court order to stop or require action — often sought for IP/confidentiality breaches

## Working with Outside Counsel
- Engagement letters: scope, fees, staffing, billing guidelines
- Privilege considerations: maintain privilege in communications
- Litigation budgeting: phase-based budgets, fee caps, alternative fee arrangements
- Reporting: regular status updates, key decision points
