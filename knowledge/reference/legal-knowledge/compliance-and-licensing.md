# Compliance & Licensing

## KYC/AML Framework

### Know Your Customer (KYC)
- Customer identification and verification
- Beneficial ownership identification
- Customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD)
- Ongoing monitoring and periodic review

### Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
- Transaction monitoring for suspicious activity
- Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) — filed when suspicious activity detected at any amount
- Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs) — filed for transactions >$10,000 (US)
- Record retention requirements (typically 5 years)

### KYB (Know Your Business)
- Business verification and entity documentation
- Beneficial ownership mapping
- Understanding of business model and expected transaction patterns
- Enhanced scrutiny for high-risk business types

## Sanctions

### Key Sanctions Regimes
- **OFAC (US)**: SDN List, Sectoral Sanctions, Country-based programs
- **EU Sanctions**: Consolidated list maintained by European Commission
- **UN Security Council**: Global sanctions programs
- **UK OFSI**: UK-specific sanctions post-Brexit

### Sanctioned Jurisdictions (as of knowledge cutoff)
- North Korea, Iran, Syria, Cuba
- Crimea, Donetsk, Luhansk regions (Russia/Ukraine)
- [Always verify current lists — sanctions change frequently]

### Sanctions in Agreements
- Representations that neither party is a sanctioned person/entity
- Screening obligations
- Right to suspend/terminate for sanctions violations
- Secondary sanctions considerations for international dealings

## Anti-Bribery / Anti-Corruption
- **FCPA (US)**: Prohibits bribing foreign government officials
- **UK Bribery Act**: Broader than FCPA — covers commercial bribery, failure to prevent
- Adequate procedures defense (UK) requires compliance programs
- Books and records requirements
- Third-party due diligence for agents and intermediaries

## Compliance in Agreements
- Mutual compliance with applicable laws representations
- Specific anti-bribery/anti-corruption representations
- Sanctions compliance representations
- Right to audit compliance programs
- Right to suspend/terminate for compliance failures
- Cooperation with regulatory examinations
- Record retention requirements
