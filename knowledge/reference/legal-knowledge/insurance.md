# Insurance

## Common Policy Types for Companies

### Directors & Officers (D&O)
- Covers directors and officers for claims arising from their management decisions
- Side A: Covers individuals when company cannot indemnify
- Side B: Reimburses company for indemnifying individuals
- Side C (Entity coverage): Covers the company for securities claims
- Critical for companies with outside investors or boards

### Errors & Omissions (E&O) / Professional Liability
- Covers claims arising from professional services or advice
- Relevant for companies providing technology services to clients
- Covers negligence, errors, and omissions in service delivery

### Cyber / Technology E&O
- Covers data breaches, cyber attacks, privacy incidents
- First-party: Incident response costs, business interruption, data recovery
- Third-party: Liability to affected individuals, regulatory fines, legal defense
- Increasingly required by partners and customers

### General Liability (CGL)
- Covers bodily injury, property damage, personal/advertising injury
- Standard for any business
- Often required by landlords, vendors, customers

### Employment Practices Liability (EPLI)
- Covers claims by employees (discrimination, harassment, wrongful termination)
- Important as headcount grows

### Crime / Fidelity
- Covers losses from employee theft, fraud, forgery
- Relevant for companies handling financial assets

## Insurance in Agreements

### When to Require Insurance from Counterparties
- Vendors with access to sensitive data → cyber insurance
- Service providers → E&O / professional liability
- Contractors on-site → general liability, workers' comp
- Financial partners → fidelity/crime insurance

### Common Requirements
- Minimum coverage amounts (varies by risk — $1M-$5M typical)
- Certificate of insurance upon request
- Named as additional insured (for GL policies)
- Notice of cancellation or material change
- Maintained throughout the term

### When Counterparties Require Insurance from Us
- Review whether we already carry the required coverage
- Check if coverage amounts are reasonable
- Verify we can provide certificates of insurance
- Flag any gaps in current coverage
