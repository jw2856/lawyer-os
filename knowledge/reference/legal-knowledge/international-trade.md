# International Trade & Export Controls

## US Export Controls
- **EAR (Export Administration Regulations)**: Administered by BIS — controls export of dual-use items
- **ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations)**: Administered by DDTC — controls defense articles
- **OFAC Sanctions**: Administers economic sanctions — SDN list, country-based programs
- **Deemed export**: Sharing controlled technology with foreign nationals in the US counts as an export

## Sanctions Regimes
- **OFAC (US)**: SDN List, Sectoral Sanctions, Country-based programs
- **EU Sanctions**: Consolidated list, maintained by European Commission
- **UN Security Council**: Global sanctions programs
- **UK OFSI**: UK-specific sanctions post-Brexit
- **Secondary sanctions**: US sanctions that apply to non-US persons (Iran, Russia programs)

## Key Restricted Jurisdictions
- Comprehensively sanctioned: North Korea, Iran, Syria, Cuba, Crimea/Donetsk/Luhansk
- Sectoral sanctions: Russia, Venezuela, certain regions/sectors
- [Always verify current lists — sanctions change frequently]

## Compliance Program Elements
- Screening of counterparties against sanctions lists (at onboarding and ongoing)
- Country/jurisdiction screening for restricted destinations
- End-use and end-user screening for controlled items
- Record keeping (5 years minimum)
- Training for relevant personnel
- Escalation procedures for potential violations
- Voluntary self-disclosure policies

## In Agreements
- Sanctions compliance representations and warranties
- Export control compliance obligations
- Right to suspend/terminate for violations
- Screening obligations
- Restricted jurisdiction exclusions
