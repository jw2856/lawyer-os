# Product Counseling

## What Product Counseling Covers

Advising product, engineering, and design teams on legal implications of product features, user-facing terms, and platform decisions. Proactive involvement in product development rather than reactive review.

## Key Areas

### Terms of Service / Terms of Use
- Governs the relationship between the platform and its users
- Must be enforceable — adequate notice and acceptance mechanism (clickwrap > browsewrap)
- Key provisions: license grant, acceptable use, disclaimers, limitation of liability, dispute resolution
- Arbitration clauses and class action waivers (enforceable per FAA, but regulatory scrutiny growing)
- Must be updated when product features change materially

### Privacy by Design
- Build privacy into product architecture, not as an afterthought
- Data minimization — only collect what's needed
- Purpose limitation — use data only for stated purposes
- Consent mechanisms — proper opt-in/opt-out flows
- Data subject rights implementation — access, deletion, portability
- Cookie consent and tracking transparency

### Feature-Specific Legal Issues
- **Payments features**: Money transmission implications, fee disclosures, error resolution
- **Messaging / communications**: Wiretap / electronic surveillance laws (ECPA), content moderation obligations
- **AI / ML features**: Bias and discrimination risks, transparency requirements, IP ownership of outputs
- **User-generated content**: DMCA safe harbor, Section 230 considerations, content moderation
- **Location data**: Enhanced privacy requirements, law enforcement access
- **Biometric data**: State-specific laws (BIPA in Illinois), consent requirements
- **Children's data**: COPPA (under 13), state laws (under 16/18)

### Accessibility
- **ADA**: Websites and apps as places of public accommodation
- **WCAG**: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines — Level AA is typical target
- **State laws**: Some states have specific digital accessibility requirements
- Risk: Demand letters and litigation for non-compliant websites/apps

### Advertising & Marketing
- Truth in advertising (FTC Act Section 5)
- Endorsement and testimonial guidelines (FTC Guides)
- CAN-SPAM (email), TCPA (calls/texts)
- Social media disclosures
- Comparative advertising limits

### Open Source
- Licensing implications of using open source in products
- Contribution policies
- License compatibility in the stack
- AGPL and copyleft risks for SaaS products

## Working with Product Teams

### When to Involve Legal
- New product or feature launch
- Changes to data collection or processing
- New monetization model
- Entering new markets or jurisdictions
- User-facing terms changes
- AI/ML feature deployment
- Third-party integrations involving user data

### How to Advise Effectively
- Participate early in product design — not just at launch
- Frame advice in terms of risk levels, not just "yes/no"
- Propose alternatives when saying "no" to a feature
- Understand the product context and business goals
- Document advice and decisions for the record
