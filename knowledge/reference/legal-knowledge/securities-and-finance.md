# Securities & Finance

## Securities Law Basics
- **Securities Act of 1933**: Governs issuance/sale of securities — registration requirement unless exempt
- **Securities Exchange Act of 1934**: Governs trading, reporting, proxy solicitation
- **Regulation D**: Private placement exemptions (Rule 504, 506(b), 506(c))
- **Rule 701**: Exemption for equity compensation plans (up to threshold amounts)
- **Regulation S**: Offshore transactions exemption
- **Regulation A/A+**: Mini-IPO for smaller offerings

## Common Exemptions for Private Companies
- **506(b)**: Unlimited accredited investors + up to 35 sophisticated non-accredited; no general solicitation
- **506(c)**: Unlimited accredited investors only; general solicitation permitted; must verify accredited status
- **Rule 701**: Compensatory equity grants to employees, directors, consultants — limited by formula

## Fundraising
- **Term sheets**: Non-binding (except exclusivity, confidentiality, governing law)
- **Stock purchase agreements**: Primary issuance documentation
- **Investor rights agreements**: Registration rights, information rights, board seats
- **Voting agreements**: Board composition, protective provisions
- **Right of first refusal / Co-sale**: Transfer restrictions on founder shares
- **409A valuations**: Required for option pricing — annually or after material events

## Equity Compensation
- **ISOs (Incentive Stock Options)**: Tax-advantaged for employees; $100K annual limit; must be exercised within 90 days of termination
- **NSOs (Non-Qualified Stock Options)**: Available to anyone; taxed as ordinary income on exercise
- **RSUs (Restricted Stock Units)**: Taxed as income upon vesting; no exercise price
- **Early exercise / 83(b) election**: File within 30 days of grant/exercise to lock in tax treatment
- **409A compliance**: Options must be granted at fair market value to avoid penalties

## Board Obligations
- **Fiduciary duties**: Duty of care (informed decisions) and duty of loyalty (no self-dealing)
- **Business judgment rule**: Protects directors who act in good faith, with care, and in company's best interest
- **D&O insurance**: Directors and officers liability coverage — standard for companies with outside investors

## Debt Financing
- **Credit agreements**: Covenants, events of default, collateral
- **Convertible notes**: Debt that converts to equity — discount rate, valuation cap, maturity
- **SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)**: Not debt — converts on triggering event
