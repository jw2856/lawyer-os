# Employment Law

## Employment Relationships
- **At-will employment**: Either party can terminate at any time, for any lawful reason (default in most US states)
- **Contractor vs employee**: Misclassification is a significant legal risk
  - IRS multi-factor test (behavioral control, financial control, relationship type)
  - ABC test (California AB5): A = free from control, B = outside usual business, C = independently established
  - Economic reality test (DOL/FLSA)
- **International employees**: Local employment law applies — significantly different protections in EU, UK, etc.

## Key Employment Agreement Terms
- **IP assignment**: Employer owns work product created in scope of employment
  - California Labor Code 2870: Cannot require assignment of inventions made on own time without employer resources
  - Similar protections in other states
- **Non-compete**: Enforceability varies dramatically by state
  - California: Generally unenforceable (Business & Professions Code 16600)
  - Many states: Enforceable if reasonable in scope, geography, and duration
  - FTC rule: Evolving federal landscape
- **Non-solicitation**: Generally more enforceable than non-competes
- **Confidentiality**: Typically survives termination
- **At-will disclaimer**: Important to preserve at-will status

## Termination
- **Documentation**: Performance issues should be documented before termination
- **Final pay**: State-specific timing requirements (California: immediate for involuntary)
- **COBRA**: Health insurance continuation notice required
- **Separation agreements**: Release of claims in exchange for severance — must comply with OWBPA for age discrimination waiver (21-day consideration, 7-day revocation)

## Equity Compensation
- **Stock options**: ISOs vs NSOs — tax treatment differs
- **RSUs**: Taxed as income upon vesting
- **409A**: Deferred compensation rules — violations result in penalties
- **Securities law**: Equity grants are securities — exemptions required (Rule 701, Reg D)
