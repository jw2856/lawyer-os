# Litigation Hold Checklist

## Trigger Assessment
- [ ] Is litigation reasonably anticipated, threatened, or pending?
- [ ] What is the nature of the dispute?
- [ ] Who are the adverse parties?
- [ ] What is the relevant time period?

## Scope Definition
- [ ] Relevant custodians identified (people who may have relevant documents)
- [ ] Relevant data sources identified (email, Slack, Drive, databases, local files)
- [ ] Relevant document types identified
- [ ] Relevant time period defined
- [ ] Relevant subject matter / keywords identified

## Hold Implementation
- [ ] Written hold notice drafted
- [ ] Hold notice distributed to all custodians
- [ ] Custodian acknowledgments received
- [ ] Auto-deletion policies suspended for relevant data
- [ ] IT notified to preserve relevant systems and backups
- [ ] Third-party vendors notified (if they hold relevant data)

## Ongoing Management
- [ ] New custodians added as identified
- [ ] Periodic reminders sent to custodians
- [ ] Hold scope updated if matter evolves
- [ ] Compliance monitored
- [ ] Hold log maintained (who was notified, when, acknowledgments)

## Release
- [ ] Matter resolved or hold no longer necessary
- [ ] Written release notice distributed
- [ ] Normal data retention policies resumed
- [ ] Hold log finalized and archived
