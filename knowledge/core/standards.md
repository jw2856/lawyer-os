# Legal Standards

> These are the **default positions** that apply to all agreement types.
> They are starting points, not absolutes. Adjust based on deal importance,
> counterparty, and business context (see `principles.md` for calibration).
>
> **Agreement-type-specific overrides** live in their respective context or playbook files:
> - Vendors / SaaS: `contexts/partners/vendors/_overview.md`
> - Customers: `contexts/partners/customers/_overview.md`
> - NDAs: `playbooks/nda-triage.md`
> - (Add new types by adding a Standards Overrides section to the relevant file)
>
> If an agreement-type file specifies a position for a clause, use that.
> If not, fall back to the defaults here.
>
> **How to fill this in**: Work through each section with real agreements.
> For each clause type, define your standard position, acceptable range,
> and escalation triggers. Build this over time -- you don't need to fill
> everything in at once.

---

## Limitation of Liability

- **Standard position**: [e.g., Mutual cap at 12 months of fees paid or payable]
- **Acceptable range**: [e.g., 12-24 months. No cap below 12 months.]
- **Required carveouts** (excluded from the cap): [e.g., fraud, gross negligence, willful misconduct, confidentiality breach, IP infringement, indemnification obligations]
- **Escalation trigger**: [e.g., Uncapped liability; missing required carveouts; asymmetric caps]

## Indemnification

- **Standard position**: [e.g., Mutual indemnification. Carved out of the liability cap.]
- **Acceptable range**: [e.g., Mutual is required. If one-sided, must include carve-out for counterparty's fault.]
- **Escalation trigger**: [e.g., Purely one-sided; no carve-out for counterparty's fault]

## Termination & Renewal

- **Standard position**: [e.g., 12-month initial term, auto-renew, 30-day non-renewal notice, 30-day cure period for material breach]
- **Acceptable range**: [e.g., Non-renewal notice 30-90 days, cure period 15-30 days]
- **Escalation trigger**: [e.g., No cure period; automatic termination without notice; early termination fees]

## Data Protection & Privacy

- **Standard position**: [e.g., Controller/processor roles defined, DPA required, breach notification within 48-72 hours, security certifications required]
- **Acceptable range**: [e.g., Breach notification up to 72 hours; either ISO 27001 or SOC 2 Type II]
- **Escalation trigger**: [e.g., No DPA when personal data is involved; no security certification; unrestricted data use]

## Confidentiality

- **Standard position**: [e.g., Mutual obligations, 5-year survival, standard exclusions]
- **Acceptable range**: [e.g., Survival of 3-5 years]
- **Escalation trigger**: [e.g., No post-termination survival; survival under 3 years]

## IP Ownership & Licensing

- **Standard position**: [e.g., Each party retains pre-existing IP; licenses limited to agreement purposes]
- **Acceptable range**: [e.g., Reasonable license grants for service delivery]
- **Escalation trigger**: [e.g., Any IP assignment clause; broad license grants; work-for-hire provisions]

## Dispute Resolution & Governing Law

- **Standard position**: [e.g., Your preferred governing law and jurisdiction]
- **Acceptable range**: [e.g., Other reasonable jurisdictions]
- **Escalation trigger**: [e.g., Exclusive foreign jurisdiction for a domestic counterparty]

## Assignment & Change of Control

- **Standard position**: [e.g., No assignment without consent; change of control triggers notification]
- **Acceptable range**: [e.g., Assignment to affiliates without consent is OK]
- **Escalation trigger**: [e.g., Unrestricted assignment rights for counterparty]
