# Policy Drafting Playbook

## When This Applies

When writing or reviewing internal policies (privacy policy, acceptable use policy, code of conduct, etc.) or external-facing terms (terms of service, terms of use).

## Required Context

- **Always**: `core/principles.md`, `core/organization.md`
- **Legal knowledge**: Relevant topic file from `reference/legal-knowledge/`
- **Situational**: Any existing version of the policy being updated

## Process

### Step 1: Define Scope and Audience

- **What type of policy?** Internal (employee-facing) or external (customer/user-facing)?
- **Who is the audience?** Employees, contractors, customers, end users, general public?
- **What law or regulation drives this?** GDPR, CCPA, employment law, industry-specific?
- **Is this a new policy or an update?** If update, get the current version first.

### Step 2: Research Requirements

- Identify legal requirements applicable to the policy topic
- Review regulatory guidance and industry best practices
- Check comparable policies from peers (if available)
- Consult with relevant internal stakeholders (HR, engineering, product, compliance)

### Step 3: Draft

- **Use plain language**: Policies should be understandable by their audience
- **Be specific**: Vague policies are unenforceable and confusing
- **Structure clearly**: Sections, headers, numbered provisions
- **Include**:
  - Purpose and scope
  - Definitions (if needed)
  - Substantive requirements/prohibitions
  - Roles and responsibilities
  - Enforcement and consequences
  - Exceptions process
  - Effective date and review cycle
  - Contact for questions

### Step 4: Review

- Legal review for compliance with applicable law
- Stakeholder review for practicality and accuracy
- Consider accessibility (reading level, translations if needed)
- Check consistency with other company policies

### Step 5: Approve and Publish

- Obtain required approvals
- Determine communication plan (how will the audience learn about the policy?)
- Publish in the appropriate location
- Set calendar reminder for periodic review

## Common Policy Types

- Privacy policy (external)
- Terms of service / terms of use (external)
- Acceptable use policy (external or internal)
- Code of conduct (internal)
- Anti-harassment policy (internal)
- Data retention policy (internal)
- Information security policy (internal)
- Whistleblower policy (internal)
- Social media policy (internal)
- Travel and expense policy (internal)
