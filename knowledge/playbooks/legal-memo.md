# Legal Memo Playbook

## When This Applies

When writing a legal memo, providing legal advice, or analyzing a legal question for internal stakeholders.

## Required Context

- **Always**: `core/principles.md`
- **By topic**: Relevant domain file(s)
- **By entity**: Relevant context file if the question relates to a specific partner/deal

## Memo Structure

```
PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL
ATTORNEY-CLIENT COMMUNICATION

## Legal Memorandum

**Date**: [date]
**To**: [stakeholder name and role]
**From**: [Your Name, Title]
**Re**: [subject — specific and descriptive]

---

### Issue

[1-2 sentences framing the question. What are we being asked to decide or advise on?]

### Background

[Relevant facts and context. Only include what's necessary to understand the analysis.
Keep it concise — the reader has context, they need analysis not storytelling.]

### Analysis

[Issue-by-issue analysis. For each sub-issue:
- State the legal principle or standard
- Apply it to our specific facts
- Reach a conclusion
- Note any assumptions or uncertainties]

### Risk Assessment

[For each identified risk:
- Severity (1-5): What's the worst case?
- Likelihood (1-5): How likely is the bad outcome?
- Risk Score: Severity x Likelihood
- Classification: GREEN (1-4) / YELLOW (5-9) / RED (10-25)]

### Recommendation

[Clear, specific recommendation. Lead with what to do, then explain why.
If there are options, present them with pros/cons and a preferred path.]

### Next Steps

[Actionable items with suggested owners and timelines:
- [ ] Action item 1 — [owner] — [by when]
- [ ] Action item 2 — [owner] — [by when]]
```

## Writing Guidelines

- **Be direct** — lead with the answer, then support it
- **Be specific** — "we should" not "one might consider"
- **Be practical** — what does this mean for the business?
- **Calibrate confidence** — distinguish between certain conclusions and judgment calls
- **Flag unknowns** — if we need more facts or research, say so upfront
- **No unnecessary hedging** — we're counseling, not covering ourselves
- **Audience-appropriate** — executives want bottom line, engineers want precision, BD wants options

## Audience Calibration

- **CEO/Board**: 1-page max. Bottom line first. Risk-calibrated language. Clear recommendation.
- **Business/BD team**: Focus on practical impact. What can they do/not do? What are the trade-offs?
- **Engineering**: Precise requirements. What exactly needs to change and why?
- **Finance**: Focus on financial exposure, liability, and compliance obligations.
- **Other lawyers**: Full analysis. Show your work. Flag open questions.
