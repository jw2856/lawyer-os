# Dispute Response Playbook

## When This Applies

When responding to a demand letter, subpoena, regulatory inquiry, litigation hold trigger, or any adversarial legal communication.

## Required Context

- **Always**: `core/principles.md`, `core/organization.md`
- **Legal knowledge**: `reference/legal-knowledge/litigation-and-disputes.md`
- **Situational**: Any existing context on the counterparty or matter

## Process

### Step 1: Assess and Classify

- **What type of dispute?** Demand letter, subpoena, regulatory inquiry, cease & desist, litigation filing, arbitration notice
- **Who is it from?** Counterparty, regulator, third party, former employee
- **What's the urgency?** Response deadlines, preservation obligations, court dates
- **Do we need outside counsel?** If litigation is filed or likely, engage outside counsel early

### Step 2: Preserve

- If litigation is reasonably anticipated, issue a **litigation hold** immediately
- Identify custodians and data sources
- Suspend auto-deletion policies for relevant data
- Document the hold and confirm compliance
- See `reference/legal-knowledge/litigation-and-disputes.md` for hold requirements

### Step 3: Investigate

- Gather relevant facts internally
- Review relevant agreements and correspondence
- Identify our exposure and potential defenses
- Assess the merits of the claim
- Determine if the matter is covered by insurance (D&O, E&O, cyber)

### Step 4: Develop Response Strategy

- **Respond, ignore, or negotiate?** Most communications require a response.
- **Tone**: Professional, factual, non-inflammatory. Don't admit liability.
- **Substance**: Address the claims specifically. Reserve rights where appropriate.
- **Timeline**: Respond within any stated deadline. If no deadline, respond within a reasonable time (typically 10-14 business days for demand letters).

### Step 5: Draft Response

- Structure depends on the type of communication
- For demand letters: acknowledge receipt, state our position, propose resolution path
- For regulatory inquiries: coordinate with compliance team, be responsive but measured
- For subpoenas: assess scope, assert applicable privileges, produce responsive documents
- Have response reviewed before sending

### Step 6: Track and Follow Up

- Log the matter in the appropriate tracker
- Set follow-up dates
- Monitor for escalation
- Update stakeholders as appropriate

## Escalation

- Active litigation → outside counsel immediately
- Regulatory enforcement action → outside counsel + executive notification
- Government subpoena → outside counsel + executive notification
- Material financial exposure → executive notification
