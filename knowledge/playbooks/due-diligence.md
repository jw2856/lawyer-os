# Due Diligence Playbook

## When This Applies

When conducting legal due diligence on a target company (M&A, investment, strategic partnership) or when responding to due diligence requests from a counterparty.

## Required Context

- **Always**: `core/principles.md`, `core/organization.md`
- **Legal knowledge**: `reference/legal-knowledge/corporate-and-governance.md` + relevant topic files
- **Situational**: Any existing context on the target or transaction

## Process — Conducting Due Diligence

### Step 1: Define Scope

- **Transaction type**: Acquisition, investment, partnership, joint venture?
- **Materiality threshold**: What level of detail is needed?
- **Key risk areas**: What matters most for this deal?
- **Timeline**: When are findings needed?
- **Access**: What data room or documents are available?

### Step 2: Request List

Standard DD categories (tailor to the transaction):
- Corporate organization (charter, bylaws, cap table, board minutes)
- Material contracts (customers, vendors, partners, leases)
- Intellectual property (patents, trademarks, copyrights, licenses, open source)
- Litigation and disputes (pending, threatened, settled)
- Regulatory and compliance (licenses, permits, investigations, consent orders)
- Employment (key employees, agreements, benefits, disputes, contractor classification)
- Real property (leases, owned property)
- Insurance (policies, claims history)
- Tax (returns, audits, disputes)
- Data privacy (policies, DPAs, breaches, compliance programs)
- Financial (audited statements, debt, liens, guarantees)

### Step 3: Review and Analyze

- Review documents systematically by category
- Flag issues by severity (critical, significant, minor, informational)
- Cross-reference findings across categories (e.g., litigation + insurance coverage)
- Identify missing documents or information gaps
- Note items requiring follow-up questions

### Step 4: Report

```
## Due Diligence Summary: [Target]

### Overall Risk Assessment: [LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH]

### Critical Findings
[Issues that could kill or restructure the deal]

### Significant Findings
[Issues that need to be addressed in the transaction documents]

### Minor Findings
[Issues to note but not deal-breakers]

### Information Gaps
[Documents or information not provided]

### Recommended Deal Protections
[Specific reps, warranties, indemnities, or conditions to include]
```

## Process — Responding to Due Diligence

### Step 1: Organize
- Set up data room or document collection
- Assign internal owners for each category
- Establish review/approval process before sharing

### Step 2: Respond
- Be responsive and organized
- Flag privileged documents — don't inadvertently waive privilege
- Track what has been shared
- Note any documents withheld and why
